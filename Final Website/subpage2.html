Extremist Groups and Threats

Within the United States of America, we have two main groups that push their form of extremism. These forms of extremism usually get divided on ideological grounds and are therefore referred to as left-wing extremism and right-wing extremism.

Left-Wing Extremism and Groups

Left-Wing Extremist Ideologies

Insurrectionary anarchism Marxist Communism Revolutionary Socialism Anarcho-Syndicalism

Left-wing extremism is made up of the politically far left people such as socialists, communists, anti-capitalists, most types of anarchists, and then a small group of social democrats and social liberals. The left-wing extremist are usually anti-government and anti-state, most appose organized religion and any religion that holds traditional beliefs, many are also extreme atheists who are hostile to most religious groups, but some are more secular in their views on religion.

Right-Wing Extremism and Groups

Right-Wing Extremist Ideologies

Reactionary Populism Identitarianism Falangism Antidisestablishmentarianism

Right-wing extremism is made up of the politically far right people such as neo-Nazis, neo-fascists, reactionaries, neo-nationalists, ethnic nationalists, and then a small group of right-wing libertarians and conservatives. The right-wing extremist have a different view of the government, they think that it needs to be “cleaned” and that it needs to be restored to its “old glory” or reconstructed to fit their view of a “true” USA. The are some, however, that are anti-government and believe that I need to be reduce to a smaller size. Within the USA many right-wing extremists are very religious, and this that fundamentalist bible teaches should be turned into the current laws that govern the USA.
